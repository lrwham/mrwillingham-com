### Example Game Ideas

Here are some examples written by past students to get you going.

You may use AI to assist in your brainstorming. You may not use AI to write your entire idea.

#### Example 1

**Title:** Astro Delivery

**Genre:** Arcade / Platformer

> You play as a space delivery driver named Rex who has to deliver packages to different planets before time runs out. The controls are WASD to move and spacebar to jump between platforms. Each planet has different gravity, so some levels you float really slow and others you fall super fast. If you drop a package or run out of time, you lose a life and have to restart that delivery. There are power-ups like rocket boots that let you double jump and a gravity belt that keeps you from floating off the screen. As you complete deliveries, the routes get longer and the planets get weirder with things like lava geysers and alien birds that try to knock packages out of your hands. The goal is to reach the highest delivery rank and unlock all the planets.

#### Example 2

**Title:** Dungeon Lunch

**Genre:** Strategy / Tower Defense

> In Dungeon Lunch, you are a goblin chef trying to feed an entire dungeon full of hungry monsters before they riot and destroy your kitchen. Monsters line up and walk toward your kitchen, and you have to drag and drop the right food onto their plate before they reach you. Each monster wants something different. Slimes want soup. Skeletons want ribs. Dragons want pizza. If you give a monster the wrong food or take too long, they get angry and your kitchen takes damage. You earn coins from happy monsters that you can use to upgrade your cooking stations and unlock new recipes. The levels get harder by adding more monster types and speeding up the line. You can play through a story mode with 30 levels or try endless mode to see how long your kitchen can survive.

{{< worksession "Group Brainstorm" >}}

Mr. Willingham will walk you through the grouping process. Once you are in your groups, you will have 20 minutes to discuss your ideas and come up with a plan for your Scratch game.

You should decide on the following as a group:
- A name for your group like a game studio name (not Bungie, Nintendo, Blizzard, but something original).
- What is the concept of your game (a platformer, a maze game, a quiz game, etc.)?
- What are the main mechanics of your game? How does the player move and interact with the game world?
- What artwork will the game need?
- What is the title of your game?
- Who is the main artist, programmer, and presenter in your group?
