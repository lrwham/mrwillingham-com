Use this rubric to guide your work and understand how your presentation will be graded. The presentation accompanies your Scratch prototype and is presented live to the class on Friday.

## Slide Content (48 points)

| Criteria | Excellent (6) | Proficient (4-5) | Developing (2-3) | Beginning (0-1) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Slide 1: Cover** | Game title, studio name, and all team members with their roles are clearly shown and well-formatted. | All required elements present. | Missing 1-2 required elements. | Missing 3+ elements or slide is blank. |
| **Slide 2: The Pitch** | One-sentence tagline, genre, and target audience are all included. The pitch is creative and clearly communicates what the game is. | All three elements present. | Missing 1 element or pitch is unclear. | Missing 2+ elements. |
| **Slide 3: Story & World** | Background story and setting are described in detail, giving the game a clear sense of place. | Story and setting are described. | Story or setting is vague or missing. | Slide missing or empty. |
| **Slide 4: Characters** | Main character, enemies, and/or key NPCs are introduced with names and descriptions. | Characters are introduced. | Only one character shown or descriptions missing. | Slide missing or empty. |
| **Slide 5: How to Play** | Controls, objective, and win/lose conditions are all clearly explained. | All three elements present. | Missing 1 element or explanation is unclear. | Missing 2+ elements. |
| **Slide 6: Concept Art** | Box art or character drawings are displayed clearly and fill the slide appropriately. | Concept art is present and visible. | Concept art present but small or hard to see. | Concept art missing. |
| **Slide 7: Live Demo** | Transition slide is present and the team smoothly switches to Scratch from here. | Transition slide present. | Transition is awkward or slide skipped. | No transition to demo. |
| **Slide 8: Credits** | All team roles listed and a working link to the shared Scratch project is included. | Credits and Scratch link present. | Missing roles or link doesn't work. | Credits slide missing. |

## Visuals & Design (12 points)

| Criteria | Excellent (6) | Proficient (4-5) | Developing (2-3) | Beginning (0-1) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Visuals on Every Slide** | Every slide has at least one image, drawing, or photo that fits the content. | Most slides have visuals. | Several slides are text-only. | Few or no visuals used. |
| **Original Artwork** | All images are original team artwork or photos of the team's drawings. | Mostly original work with minor exceptions. | Mix of original and borrowed images. | Uses clip art, internet images, or characters from other games. |

## Presentation Delivery (20 points)

| Criteria | Excellent (5) | Proficient (3-4) | Developing (1-2) | Beginning (0) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Everyone Speaks** | Every team member speaks for at least one slide. | All members speak, but some briefly. | One member does most of the talking. | Only one person presents. |
| **Speaks Clearly** | All presenters speak loudly, clearly, and at a good pace. Eye contact is made with the audience. | Presenters speak clearly with minor issues. | Presenters speak too quietly, too fast, or mumble. | Presenters are inaudible. |
| **Doesn't Read Slides** | Presenters talk to the audience and explain ideas in their own words. | Presenters mostly talk naturally with occasional reading. | Presenters read most of the slides word-for-word. | Presenters read every slide directly. |
| **Length (3-5 minutes)** | Presentation runs 3-5 minutes for slides, plus live demo. | Runs slightly over or under (within 30 seconds). | Runs 1+ minute over or under target. | Significantly off target or not timed. |

## Live Scratch Demo (20 points)

| Criteria | Excellent (10) | Proficient (7-9) | Developing (4-6) | Beginning (0-3) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Game is Playable** | Scratch project is shared publicly, opens without issues, and the main mechanic works during the demo. | Game works with minor bugs. | Game has noticeable bugs or team struggles to run it. | Game does not work or project was not shared. |
| **Explains the Game While Demoing** | Team narrates what's happening, shows the main features, and handles any issues calmly. | Team explains the demo clearly. | Demo is silent or confusing. | No demo given or team gives up. |
