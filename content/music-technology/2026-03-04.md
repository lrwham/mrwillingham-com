# Before you begin...

Before you begin today's lesson, read the entirety of the instructions below for the warmup, work session, and closing. Then, begin the warmup.

## Today's Objectives

- I can setup a microphone and audio interface for recording.
- I can record a sample podcast intro using Soundtrap.
- I can listen back to my recording and identify areas for improvement.

---

## 🔥 Warmup: Read the Sample Script

Read the entire [sample script](../sample-podcast-script/) silently. As you read, imagine how it would sound if you were saying it out loud. Pay attention to the flow of the sentences and the overall structure.

### ✅ Warmup Checkpoint

I have read the script.

## 🎙️ Work Session: Review Microphone Procedures and Record

We'll discuss the setup procedures and recording techniques for using a microphone and audio interface. Then, you'll record a sample podcast intro using Soundtrap.

Key Points to Remember:
- Always check your gain levels before recording.
- Sit about 4–6 inches from the microphone for optimal sound quality.
- Speak clearly and confidently.

We'll take turns being the audio engineer and the talent.

### Audio Engineer Responsibilities:
- Set up the microphone and audio interface.
- Monitor the recording levels and adjust as needed.

### Talent Responsibilities:
- Read the sample script with expression and clarity.
- Follow the directions in the script for tone.
- Don't worry about mistakes — you can always edit later

---

## 🎧 Closing: Listen to your Recording

After recording your sample podcast intro, take some time to listen back to it. Pay attention to the clarity of your voice, the pacing, and any background noise.
