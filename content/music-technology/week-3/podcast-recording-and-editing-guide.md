## Recording Your Podcast

### Setting Up Your Space

Before you hit record, make sure your environment is as quiet as possible.

- Find a quiet spot away from hallways, vents, and other people talking.
- Close doors and windows to block outside noise.
- Put your phone on silent so notifications don't interrupt your recording.
- Soft surfaces (like curtains, carpet, or even a hoodie draped nearby) help reduce echo.

### Microphone Technique

How you use your microphone makes a big difference in sound quality.

- Position yourself about **6 to 8 inches** from the mic — roughly a fist's distance away.
- Speak **across** the mic, not directly into it. This reduces popping sounds on words that start with "P" or "B."
- Keep a **consistent distance** from the mic. Don't lean in and out while you talk.
- Avoid tapping the table or touching the mic while recording.

### Speaking Clearly

- Speak at a **steady, natural pace** — slightly slower than a normal conversation.
- Pronounce your words clearly. Don't mumble or rush through sentences.
- It's okay to pause and **re-record a section**. That's what editing is for!
- Keep water nearby to avoid dry mouth.

## Editing Your Podcast

### Cleaning Up Your Recording

- **Listen through your entire recording** before making any cuts. Take notes on what needs to be fixed.
- Remove long pauses, "umms," "uhhs," and false starts.
- Cut out any background noise or interruptions.
- Use **fade-in and fade-out** effects to smooth transitions between cuts so they don't sound abrupt.

### Adjusting Audio Levels

- Make sure your voice is at a **consistent volume** throughout the podcast. If one part is too quiet or too loud, adjust it.
- Music and sound effects should be **quieter than your voice**. Your audience is there to hear you talk — the music supports you, it shouldn't overpower you.
- Use the **volume controls** in Soundtrap to balance your different tracks.

### Adding Music and Sound Effects

- Place your **intro music** at the beginning and fade it out before you start talking (or lower it under your voice).
- Add **transitions** between major sections to help your podcast flow smoothly.
- Use sound effects **sparingly**. They should support your content, not distract from it.
- Add **outro music** at the end to wrap up your podcast.

## The Podcast Workflow: Start to Finish

Here is the full process from start to finish:

1. **Write your script** (already done!)
2. **Set up your recording space** — find a quiet spot and position your mic.
3. **Record your voice tracks** following your script.
4. **Listen back** and take notes on what needs to be fixed.
5. **Edit** — remove mistakes, trim silences, and adjust levels.
6. **Add music and sound effects** to enhance your podcast.
7. **Listen to the full podcast** from start to finish one more time.
8. **Export as MP3** and share!

> Professional podcasters rarely get a perfect take on the first try. The editing process is where a good podcast becomes a **great** podcast.
