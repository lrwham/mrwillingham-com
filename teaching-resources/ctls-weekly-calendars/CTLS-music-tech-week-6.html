Music Technology — Week 6

Unit: Musical Form and Melody Writing

This week we learn to write melodies using common musical forms. You'll explore ABA form, binary form, and 32-bar (AABA) form, and how chord progressions support a melody. By Friday you'll have a finished AABA project with chords, drums, and an original melody exported as an MP3.

Day Date Topic Summary
26 Mon 4/27 ABA Form and the Tonic Note Learn ABA form and the tonic note, then compose an original melody in C major using MuseScore.
27 Tue 4/28 Binary Form and Bass Lines Identify and mark A and B sections in a binary-form melody in MuseScore, then add a bass line in C major or A minor.
28 Wed 4/29 Chord Progressions and Melody Explore real chord progressions on Hooktheory, then compose an 8-measure A section in C major and an 8-measure B section in A minor with a melody on top.
29 Thu 4/30 32-Bar Form and Chords in GarageBand Import MIDI chord progressions, arrange them into AABA (32-bar) form in GarageBand, add a drum loop, explore the arpeggiator, and begin a melody.
