## Music Technology

aligned to Georgia Standards of Excellence (MSMTC8) across the Creating, Performing, Responding, and Connecting domains.

Teaches students to use digital tools to create, perform, respond to, and connect to music as both an art form and an industry, preparing them for high school music/media technology courses.

45 days across 9 weeks

## Units so far...

### Unit 1: Podcasts (Days 1–11)
Students create a complete 3–5 minute podcast episode from concept to distribution.

- **Week 1 — Foundations (Days 1–5):** Computer lab basics and OneDrive setup; sound wave science via BrainPop (frequency, amplitude, compression, rarefaction); microphone and audio interface setup with gain adjustment; podcast topic brainstorming; practice recording and topic selection.
- **Week 2 — Production (Days 6–10):** Writing a 250–500 word script with audio annotations; creating original 10–20 second intro music using GarageBand loops; peer feedback and script revision; full recording in Soundtrap; editing, level balancing, sound effects, MP3 export, and class sharing.
- **Week 3 — Completion (Day 11):** Final edits, export, and class listening session with peer comments.

### Unit 2: Sound Design (Days 12–15)
Introduction to sound design principles using GarageBand with video and animation.

- **Day 12 — Rocket League Sound Design:** First application of effects (Pitch Shift, Reverb, EQ, Distortion) to video.
- **Day 13 — Agent 327: Operation Barbershop:** Prepared sound libraries, automation, and music layering for a 30-second short film.
- **Day 14 — Sound Design From Scratch:** Recording found sounds with portable recorders and processing with plugins (Reverb, Delay, Distortion, Pitch Shifter, EQ, Chorus, Tremolo).
- **Day 15 — Minecraft Sound Design:** Collaborative whole-class sound design activity.

### Tools and Techniques
- **DAWs:** GarageBand (primary), Soundtrap (for podcast recording with built-in noise reduction).
- **Hardware:** Mac computers, XLR microphones, audio interfaces, portable audio recorders.
- **Skills:** Microphone technique, gain staging, loop-based composition, tempo/key matching, editing (trim, split, level automation), sound effect placement, MP3 export.
- **Effects taught:** Pitch Shift, Reverb, Delay, Distortion, EQ, Chorus, Tremolo.
